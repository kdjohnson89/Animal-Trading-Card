Atlantic Puffin

The Atlantic Puffin is a black and white bird that nests on coastal cliffs or offshore islands.

  • Scientific name: Fratercula arctica
  • Life span: 20+ years
  • Location: North Atlantic
  • Size: 10 inches

Spending most of their life at sea, they rest by floating on waves. A puffin can dive up to 200 ft. Small fish are typically hunted, including herring and sand eels.
